Audience

WHO READS THIS PAGE

Primary
Fabric Manufacturers

Companies that make curtain and blind fabrics and need marketing assets to sell them into retail or direct to trade buyers. They have ranges, colourways and header styles — but no room imagery to show them in context.

Need to show their full range, not just hero pieces
Frustrated by the cost and lead time of photography
Want images sized for web, catalogue and e-commerce
Secondary
Retail Brands

Retailers selling curtains and blinds who commission their own product imagery for online and catalogue use. They need to show ranges in styled environments without running a shoot for every colourway.

Speed to market on new season ranges
Consistency across a large product set
Cost reduction vs. traditional photography
The offering

HOW IT WORKS

Client sends
Swatch

A colour-accurate reference file or a physical sample. The photograph of the swatch they are happy with sets the colour we work to.

→
Image Foundry builds
CGI Set

Curtains or blinds matched to the fabric — weave, drape, header style — placed in a styled room. Multiple rooms, colourways and lighting from one foundation.

→
Client receives
Assets

Marketing-ready imagery in days. Sized for web, catalogue, e-commerce and social. No set build, no sample production, no reshoots.

Key point for the page No AI mention on page. The message is speed and cost, not the tech behind it. The proof is in the imagery itself.

Section by section

WHAT EACH SECTION DOES

S1
Hero

Full-screen room image with curtains or blinds. Headline punches the core offer. Subhead is one sentence. This is the only section that does not need to explain — the image does the work.

Image: hero portfolio shot — curtains styled in room, full bleed
S2
The Problem

Sets up the pain. Photography for soft furnishings is expensive, slow and demands sample production before a single shot. The reader should feel recognised here — this is their problem.

Image: swatch / design table — works well as a contrast to the hero
S3
What We Do

Flipped 50/50 — image leads left this time, breaking the visual rhythm. Short, confident copy. The headline does the job: "We build the finished look." No over-explanation.

Image: CGI curtains — styled room, clear and clean
S4
How It Works

Three cards on a light-grey ground. Each step is blunt: send swatch, we build it, you get assets. Middle card has a teal top bar to break the monotony. No waffle.

No images needed — copy-driven section
S5
Why It Sells

Left side makes the emotional case — buyers decide faster when they can see the finished result. Right side lists three concrete outputs: one fabric every room, every colourway one cost, finish variations.

No images — left col is text, right col is three stacked points
S6
Colourway Panel
→

The commercial proof. Same room scene, two colourways side by side. Arrow between them. Label below reads "Same CGI scene / Different colourway — same cost." This single image makes the cost argument better than any copy.

Must-have images: two colourway variants of the same fabric in the same room
S7
Gallery — 12-column showreel grid

Six images in a deliberately uneven 12-column grid: spans of 5-4-3 / 3-5-4. The asymmetry prevents the showreel looking like a uniform tile grid. Each cell carries a product-type tag (Pencil Pleat, Roller Blinds etc) and reveals a hover overlay. The gallery is the proof of range — it should show six distinctly different room types and/or product styles.

6 images needed — curtains and blinds, different room types, different header/finish styles
